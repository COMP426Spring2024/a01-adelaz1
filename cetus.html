Cetus was sent by the sea god Poseidon to punish the kingdom of Ethiopia, as a consequence of Queen Cassiopeia's vanity, who boasted that her daughter Andromeda was more beautiful than the Nereids, the sea nymphs. To appease Poseidon, the kingdom's oracle advised sacrificing Andromeda. Andromeda was chained to a rock as an offering, but just before Cetus could devour her, the hero Perseus arrived. Perseus used Medusa's severed head to turn Cetus to stone, rescuing Andromeda and saving the kingdom.
