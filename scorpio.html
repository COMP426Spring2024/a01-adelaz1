The legend of Scorpio is deeply rooted in Greek mythology and centers around the story of Orion and the Scorpion. According to the myth, Orion was a mighty hunter who boasted of his hunting prowess, claiming he could slay any creature on Earth. Gaia, the Earth goddess, grew angry at Orion's arrogance and sent a giant scorpion to confront him. The scorpion stung Orion, fatally wounding him. Orion and the scorpion were placed in the sky as constellations by Zeus to honor their battle and eternal struggle. Thus, the legend of Scorpio symbolizes themes of hubris, fate, and the balance of power in the cosmos.
