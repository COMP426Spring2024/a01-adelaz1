The constellation Draco is often associated with Ladon, a serpent from Greek mythology. Ladon guarded the golden apples in the Garden of the Hesperides, which were a wedding gift to Hera. Ladon was slain by Heracles (Hercules) during his Twelve Labors, as he was tasked with retrieving the golden apples. Ladon's presence in the sky as the constellation Draco symbolizes the mythical serpent's role as a guardian and the heroism of Heracles in overcoming formidable challenges in his quests.
